27    /**
28    * Interval is the standard implementation of an immutable time interval.
29    * <p>
30    * A time interval represents a period of time between two instants.
31    * Intervals are inclusive of the start instant and exclusive of the end.
32    * The end instant is always greater than or equal to the start instant.
33    * <p>
34    * Intervals have a fixed millisecond duration.
35    * This is the difference between the start and end instants.
36    * The duration is represented separately by {@link ReadableDuration}.
37    * As a result, intervals are not comparable.
38    * To compare the length of two intervals, you should compare their durations.
39    * <p>
40    * An interval can also be converted to a {@link ReadablePeriod}.
41    * This represents the difference between the start and end points in terms of fields
42    * such as years and days.
43    * <p>
